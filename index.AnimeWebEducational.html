  1. Dr.Stone

    2. "Dr. Stone" is a Japanese anime and manga series set in a world where a mysterious event turns all of humanity into stone statues. Thousands of years later, Senku Ishigami, a brilliant high school student, emerges and uses his scientific knowledge to rebuild civilization. The story revolves around Senku's quest to revive humanity, exploring science, technology, and survival in a post-apocalyptic world. The series blends adventure, humor, and educational elements, showcasing Senku's inventive spirit as he unlocks the secrets of science to overcome challenges and advance society.

  2. Cells at Work

    3. "Cells at Work!" ("Hataraku Saibou") is a Japanese anime and manga series that takes a creative and entertaining approach to biology by personifying cells within the human body. Created by Akane Shimizu, the series presents the inner workings of the human body as a bustling city inhabited by anthropomorphic cells. The main characters include Red Blood Cell (AE3803) and White Blood Cell (U-1146), among others, each representing different types of cells with distinct roles and functions. The narrative follows their daily activities as they work together to maintain the body's health, fight against infections, and address various health challenges. "Cells at Work!" provides a fun and educational way to learn about human physiology, immune responses, and the functions of different cells. The anime combines humor, action, and informative content, making it accessible to a wide audience, including those with an interest in biology or medicine. In addition to its educational value, the series explores teamwork, resilience, and the importance of each cell's contribution to maintaining the body's balance. The success of "Cells at Work!" lies in its ability to present complex biological concepts in an engaging and visually appealing manner.

  3. Great Teacher Onizuka

    4. "Great Teacher Onizuka" follows Eikichi Onizuka, a former gang member and motorcycle gang leader who becomes an unconventional high school teacher. His unorthodox methods and carefree attitude help him connect with students, challenging societal norms and addressing various issues affecting teenagers. Onizuka's journey involves humor, drama, and heartfelt moments as he strives to make a positive impact on his students' lives and redefine what it means to be a great teacher. The series explores themes of education, personal growth, and the importance of understanding and connecting with others.

  4. Silver Spoon

    5. "Silver Spoon," written and illustrated by Hiromu Arakawa, is a Japanese manga and anime series. The story follows Yugo Hachiken, a city boy who enrolls in Ooezo Agricultural High School to escape the academic pressures of his urban life. Initially ignorant about farming and rural life, Hachiken learns about the challenges and rewards of agriculture as he interacts with his classmates and the school's animals. The series explores themes of hard work, the agricultural industry, and the pursuit of one's passion. "Silver Spoon" combines humor with insightful commentary on the food industry, education, and the choices individuals make in pursuit of their goals. As Hachiken adapts to the agricultural environment and forms bonds with his peers, the series provides a unique perspective on the importance of agriculture in society.

  5. Moyashimon

    6. "Moyashimon" is a Japanese manga and anime series created by Masayuki Ishikawa. The story follows Tadayasu Sawaki, a first-year student at an agricultural university who possesses the unique ability to see and communicate with microorganisms, including bacteria and yeast. This ability is represented by the microorganisms appearing as cute, anthropomorphic characters. Sawaki's extraordinary talent brings him into contact with various aspects of microbiology and the agricultural sciences. As he navigates university life, he becomes involved in agricultural projects, research, and the challenges of working with microorganisms in different contexts. The series combines comedy, slice-of-life elements, and educational content, offering insights into microbiology and the role of microorganisms in various industries, including food fermentation and agriculture. "Moyashimon" explores themes of friendship, personal growth, and the fascinating world of microorganisms in an engaging and lighthearted manner.

  6. March Comes in Like a Lion

    7. "March Comes in Like a Lion" ("3-gatsu no Lion") is a Japanese anime and manga series created by Chica Umino. The story revolves around Rei Kiriyama, a young professional shogi player who battles loneliness, depression, and the weight of his past. Despite his success in the world of professional shogi, Rei struggles with personal demons and a sense of isolation. The series delves into Rei's relationships with fellow shogi players, as well as his connection with the Kawamoto sisters—Akari, Hinata, and Momo—who offer him warmth and support. As Rei navigates the competitive world of shogi and confronts his emotional struggles, the series explores themes of self-discovery, mental health, and the importance of human connections. "March Comes in Like a Lion" is celebrated for its emotional depth, stunning animation, and its sensitive portrayal of complex characters. It captures the challenges and triumphs of life, blending drama, slice-of-life elements, and introspective storytelling to create a moving and thought-provoking narrative.

  7. Shirobako

    8. "Shirobako" is a Japanese anime series that provides a behind-the-scenes look at the anime industry. Created by Tsutomu Mizushima and Michiko Yokote, the story follows five friends—Aoi Miyamori, Ema Yasuhara, Shizuka Sakaki, Misa Toudou, and Midori Imai—who aspire to work in the anime industry. After graduating high school, they pursue different roles within the industry, such as animation, voice acting, and production. Aoi Miyamori, the protagonist, lands a job at Musashino Animation, a fictional anime studio, where she faces the challenges and demands of producing an anime series. "Shirobako" explores the creative process, industry dynamics, and the trials faced by the characters as they work together to overcome various obstacles in anime production. The series offers insights into the roles of animators, voice actors, producers, and other staff involved in bringing an anime from concept to completion. With a mix of comedy, drama, and industry commentary, "Shirobako" provides an engaging and informative portrayal of the anime-making process.

  8. Bakuman

    9. "Bakuman" is a Japanese manga and anime series created by Tsugumi Ohba and Takeshi Obata. The story revolves around Moritaka Mashiro and Akito Takagi, two high school students with dreams of becoming a successful manga artist and writer, respectively. The narrative begins when Mashiro, a talented artist, accidentally discovers Takagi's desire to become a manga writer. The two decide to team up and pursue their dreams together. Their journey involves entering the competitive world of manga publishing, facing challenges, and working towards the goal of having their manga serialized and eventually adapted into an anime. "Bakuman" provides a detailed and realistic depiction of the manga industry, exploring the creative process, editorial decisions, and the competitive nature of the field. The series delves into themes of ambition, perseverance, and the sacrifices artists make to achieve success in the world of manga. With a blend of drama, humor, and insights into the manga creation process, "Bakuman" offers an engaging and informative narrative for both manga enthusiasts and those interested in the creative industry.

  9. Planetes

    10. "Planetes" is a Japanese anime and manga series that follows the crew of the debris collection ship, Toy Box, in the near future. The story is set in space, where space debris poses a significant threat to spacecraft. The main characters, Hachirota "Hachimaki" Hoshino and Ai Tanabe, work for the Technora Corporation, tasked with collecting and disposing of debris in Earth's orbit. The series explores themes of human ambition, the challenges of space exploration, and the impact of space debris on future space travel. "Planetes" blends hard science fiction elements with character-driven narratives, delving into the personal and professional lives of the crew as they navigate the complexities of their space missions and the relationships that develop among them. The anime is known for its realistic portrayal of space technology, politics, and the human experience in outer space.

  10. Space Brothers

    11. "Space Brothers" ("Uchuu Kyoudai") is a Japanese manga and anime series written and illustrated by Chuya Koyama. The story revolves around two brothers, Mutta and Hibito Nanba, who share a childhood dream of becoming astronauts and traveling to space. The narrative begins with Mutta, the older brother, facing challenges in his career and personal life. After a series of setbacks, Mutta decides to pursue his childhood dream of becoming an astronaut, just like his younger brother Hibito, who is already on the path to becoming one. The series follows Mutta's journey through the rigorous selection process at the Japan Aerospace Exploration Agency (JAXA) and his experiences as he trains to become an astronaut. "Space Brothers" combines elements of drama, comedy, and science fiction. It explores themes of perseverance, the pursuit of dreams, and the bond between siblings. The series is known for its realistic portrayal of the astronaut selection process, space exploration, and the challenges faced by individuals striving to achieve their goals. It offers a mix of heartwarming moments and inspirational storytelling.
